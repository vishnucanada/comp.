Governance that lives inside the model

Traditional output filters can be bypassed. comp. restructures the model itself so restricted capabilities are mathematically unreachable at low privilege.

How it works

From policy to enforcement in four steps

No prompt engineering. No output filters. The model itself changes behavior.

1
Write your policy in plain English
Open the Admin Dashboard and describe what the model should and shouldn't reveal — in natural language, the same way you'd write it for a compliance document or HR memo.
PolicyTranslator
2
Claude compiles it into structured rules
The translator calls Claude to extract DENY and ALLOW categories with matching keywords and regex patterns. You can inspect the compiled policy in the dashboard before proceeding.
PolicyCompiler
3
Wrap your model with NLPN layers
A single wrap_with_nlpn(model) call replaces all target Linear layers with NLPNLinear — a rank-factorized weight that supports nested subspaces. Initialized from your existing weights via SVD, so no capability is lost at rmax.
NLPNLinear
4
Train and certify
Policy-targeted training teaches the model that denied capabilities are genuinely unreachable at low privilege — not just filtered. The verifier then issues a compliance certificate confirming suppression and preservation thresholds are met.
CertificateVerifier
